About Us

What is Common Ground?

There's more that connects us than we think. Shared interests, shared questions, shared hopes — but we live so isolated. Different schedules, different screens, different routines. We pass each other by.

Common Ground is a social club built on that idea. We bring people together around what they love — clubs and experiences where that common ground becomes real. Not online. In person. With people who get it.

Connection first, activity second. The activity is simply the excuse.

We curate who's in the room. Small groups. Weekly clubs so you see the same faces. No apps, no networking — just the structure for connection. You show up. The rest follows.

Because the best parts of life — friendship, laughter, shared experiences — were never meant to happen alone.

Our mission: To make meeting new people in real life normal again. Launching in the Bay Area — more cities coming soon.

Origin Story

Why I Built This

Hi, I'm Liam.

For most of my life, meeting people and building friendships came naturally to me. I've always loved connecting with others, hearing people's stories, and bringing people together.

But a few years ago my life changed in ways I didn't expect. First the pandemic disrupted everything. Then I had a serious accident that forced me into a long recovery. I spent months mostly indoors, disconnected from the routines and social life I once had.

Without realizing it, my world got smaller and my circle of friends slowly faded. For the first time in my life, I experienced something a lot of adults quietly struggle with: loneliness and isolation.

My confidence dropped. Even simple social situations started to feel intimidating. I found myself avoiding life more than living it.

The only way out was connection. The thing I'd been avoiding—and the thing I craved most.

So I started small. I began getting out of the house again. Going on walks. Doing things alone in public. Starting small conversations with strangers. At first it felt uncomfortable — almost like exposure therapy. But slowly something shifted. I started meeting people again. My confidence came back. Life started to open up again. And I realized how many adults are quietly going through the same thing.

People want connection. They want friendships. They want people to explore life with. But once you're out of school and settled into routines, it can be surprisingly hard to meet new people and build meaningful relationships.

That realization is what led me to create Common Ground.

A social club where strangers meet through shared experiences and build real friendships.

Because rebuilding your social life shouldn't feel impossible.

And if you're trying to expand your circle again, you're not the only one.

I'm doing it too.

Common Ground simply gives us a place to start.

Common Ground is built on respect, presence, and genuine connection.

By joining a club, you agree to:

  • Be respectful — Treat everyone with kindness. Harassment or discrimination will not be tolerated.
  • Be present — Show up on time and engage with the experience.
  • Respect boundaries — Everyone's comfort level is different. Honor that.
  • Keep it sober-friendly — Our gatherings are designed for connection without alcohol.
  • No solicitation — This is not a place for business pitches or promotions.

Anyone who violates these guidelines may be asked to leave.

## 8. The Quiz

### Core Promise

**Your relationships are not random. There's a pattern behind them. This quiz reveals it.**

### What the Quiz Must Feel Like

- **Not** a test
- **A diagnosis of her love life**
- When she sees the result: *"That explains everything."*

### The Real Job of the Quiz

**Restoring self-trust in romantic decisions.**

That's the deeper psychological transformation.

### The 3 Things the Quiz Must Deliver

1. **"Oh shit that's me" moment**
   - Result names should feel personal and slightly uncomfortable in a truthful way
   - Examples: The Over-Investing Lover, The Fixer, The Emotional Caretaker, The Hopeful Romantic, The Avoider, The Loyalty Trapper
   - That's what makes people share quizzes

2. **Explain why her relationships repeat**
   - Pattern → Behavior → Relationship Outcome
   - Example: "You tend to give too much too early, which attracts emotionally unavailable men who enjoy the attention but struggle with commitment."

3. **Reveal the type of man this pattern attracts**
   - Example: Your pattern: The Over-Investor. Men it often attracts: emotionally unavailable men, men who like attention but avoid commitment, men who enjoy being pursued.
   - This connects to your male perspective advantage.

### Quiz Structure (5 Steps)

1. **Behavior questions** about relationships — "When you start liking someone…", "When a partner pulls away…", "What role do you usually play?"
2. **Reveal the pattern type** — Name + what it means, why it developed, how it shows up
3. **Explain the relationship loop** — "You tend to attract X because you naturally do Y"
4. **Show the cost of the pattern** — Dating choices, emotional energy, relationship outcomes (builds urgency)
5. **Introduce the next step** — "How do I break this?" → paid offer

### Emotional Experience the Quiz Should Create

1. **Curiosity** — "Maybe this explains my dating life."
2. **Recognition** — "That is exactly what I do."
3. **Relief** — "So I'm not crazy."
4. **Motivation** — "I want to stop repeating this."

### Emotional Payoff

When she finishes, she should feel:
- Understood
- Relieved
- Curious about changing

**The thought:** *"That explains my entire dating life."* — That's when people share quizzes.

### The Hook (What Works)

**Not:** "Take this before you lose time on him" (sounds like advice)

**Yes:** "This explains why this keeps happening to you."

### Problem Copy (Landing Page)

> Do your relationships tend to follow the same pattern?
>
> • You start excited about someone…
> • Things feel promising at first…
> • Then something shifts…
> • And suddenly you're back in the same situation again.
>
> If this feels familiar, you're not alone.
>
> Many women unknowingly repeat relationship patterns that influence who they attract and how relationships unfold.

### Audience Feedback on Quiz

Women like the pattern concept but say: *"The pattern is interesting, but I also want to know what it means for the man I'm with."*

They want:
- What type of men this attracts
- How to recognize them
- What to do differently

**That's the missing bridge.**

### The True Job of the Quiz

The quiz is not the product. The quiz is a **mirror**.

It should make her realize:
- Why relationships repeat
- What role she plays in them
- Why certain men show up

Once she sees that, she wants to break the pattern. That's where your program comes in.

## 11. Content Strategy

### Your Best Content Format

> "Women think this means he likes them…"
>
> "But to most men it actually means this."

That's translation. That's why your content works.

### The 12 Relationship Questions Women Search at Night

**Category 1 — "Does He Actually Like Me?"**
1. Why does he pull away when things start getting serious?
2. If a man likes you, how does he act?
3. Why is he hot and cold with me?
4. Why does he text me every day but never commit?

**Category 2 — "Am I Being Played?"**
5. How do you know if a man is wasting your time?
6. What are the signs he's not serious about you?
7. Why would a man keep you around if he doesn't want a relationship?

**Category 3 — "Is It Me?"**
8. Why do I keep attracting emotionally unavailable men?
9. Why do my relationships always start great and end badly?
10. Why do I keep choosing the wrong men?

**Category 4 — "Should I Leave?"**
11. How do you know when it's time to walk away from a relationship?
12. Can emotionally unavailable men change?

### The Question That Could Become Your Brand

**"What does his behavior actually mean?"**

That question alone can generate hundreds of videos, multiple products, quizzes, courses.

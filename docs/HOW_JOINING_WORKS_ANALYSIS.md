# How Joining Works — Analysis & Rewrite

## 1. Product Accuracy Check ✓

The current 4 steps match the actual flow:
- **Apply** → Form collects name, email, city, age, interests, why join
- **Receive invites** → Email/text to clubs + experiences
- **Choose & reserve** → Pick gatherings, pay $15–40 to confirm
- **Show up** → Group chat before, guided introductions at event

One minor mismatch: The headline says "Apply. Get Invited. Show Up." (3 actions) but steps show 4. "Choose and reserve" is implied in "Get Invited" but could confuse.

---

## 2. Section Goal

**Primary:** Convert visitors to apply — reduce friction, answer objections, build trust.

**Secondary:** Set expectations so applicants know what happens next (reduces anxiety, builds trust).

---

## 3. Shortcomings Against Conversion Goal

| Shortcoming | Impact |
|-------------|--------|
| **Cognitive load** | 4 steps × 4 paragraphs = heavy. Users skim. Verbose copy loses mobile readers. |
| **Price buried** | $15–40 only in step 3. Market research: price visibility early increases trust; competitors show pricing. |
| **Anxiety not addressed** | "We guide introductions" is buried in step 4. Target audience (isolated adults, new to Bay Area) fears awkwardness — this reassurance should land earlier. |
| **Headline/step mismatch** | "Apply. Get Invited. Show Up." = 3 actions. Steps = 4. Align for clarity. |
| **Objections incomplete** | "Free to apply" ✓. "Pay only when you go" ✓. Missing: "What if I'm not accepted?" (could add light reassurance). |
| **Differentiators underplayed** | Curated (not algorithmic), pay-per-attend (no subscription), come solo — these beat competitors but aren't emphasized. |
| **Repetition** | "Apply once. Get ongoing invitations" appears in hero, What We Offer, and form. Intro here can differentiate. |

---

## 4. Psychology & Market Insights (from docs)

- **Clarity beats creativity** — obvious signals convert
- **Low friction** — 30–50% abandon on long forms; same applies to long copy
- **Emotional outcomes** — "meeting new people feels natural" > transactional benefits
- **Trust** — structure, transparency, "what happens next"
- **Bay Area audience** — tired of apps, wants real-life; ritual ("same time, same place") resonates
- **Pay-per-attend** — key differentiator vs subscription competitors

---

## 5. Rewrite Strategy

1. **Collapse to 3 steps** — Match headline. "Choose and reserve" folds into "Get Invited" (invites include reserve link).
2. **Front-load benefits** — Free to apply. Pay $15–40 only when you attend.
3. **Lead with anxiety reducer** — "Come solo. We guide introductions." early.
4. **Shorter copy** — One clear sentence per step. Optional second line only if needed.
5. **Intro refresh** — Different from "Apply once. Get ongoing invitations" — focus on ease + low commitment.

---

## 6. Proposed Copy

**Section label:** How Joining Works  
**Headline:** Apply. Get Invited. Show Up.  
**Intro:** Free to apply. Pay $15–40 only when you attend. Come solo — we guide introductions.

**Step 1 — Apply**  
One application. Tell us about yourself and what interests you. We'll send invites that match.

**Step 2 — Get invited**  
Weekly email and text invites to clubs and experiences. Pick what you want, pay to reserve your spot.

**Step 3 — Show up**  
Connect with attendees in a group chat before the gathering. We guide introductions so meeting people feels natural.

---

## 7. Rationale for Changes

- **3 steps** — Matches headline; less cognitive load.
- **Intro** — Price + "come solo" + "we guide introductions" upfront. Addresses cost objection and social anxiety in one line.
- **Step 1** — Shorter. "Invites that match" = curated, not random.
- **Step 2** — Combines receive + choose + pay. "Pay to reserve" = clear action.
- **Step 3** — Group chat + guided intros. Outcome-focused: "feels natural."

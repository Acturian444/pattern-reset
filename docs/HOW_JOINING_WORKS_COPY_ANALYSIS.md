# How Joining Works — Copy Analysis & Rewrite

## 1. Section Goal

**Primary:** Convert visitors to apply — reduce friction, answer objections, build trust.

**Secondary:** Set expectations so applicants know what happens next (reduces anxiety, builds trust).

**Success =** Visitor reads, feels "I get it" and "I want to do this," then clicks Apply.

---

## 2. Shortcomings Against Goal

| Shortcoming | Current | Impact |
|-------------|---------|--------|
| **Cognitive overload** | Intro packs 5 ideas into one dense block. Step 3 is 4 sentences. | Users skim and miss key points. Mobile readers drop off. |
| **Redundancy** | "You cover your own food and drinks" / "pay for your own meal or drinks" appears in intro AND step 2. | Feels repetitive. Wastes attention. |
| **Negative framing** | "You cover your own" / "you'll pay for your own" — sounds like a caveat. | Undermines trust. Could be neutral or benefit-framed. |
| **Jargon** | "Curated lists" — internal language. | Confusing. "We match you to the right gatherings" is clearer. |
| **Differentiators buried** | Group chat with bios, follow-up without trading numbers — huge differentiators. | Lost in Step 3's length. Should land harder. |
| **Anxiety reducer late** | "Come solo — we guide introductions" at end of intro. | Target audience fears awkwardness. Lead with it. |
| **Step 3 too long** | 4 distinct ideas in one paragraph. | Hard to scan. Key benefit (group chat) competes with others. |
| **No emotional payoff** | Copy is functional. | "Meeting people feels natural" and "follow up without trading numbers" are emotional — could land stronger. |

---

## 3. What's Working

- **Headline** — "Apply. Get Invited. Show Up." — Clear, scannable, matches 3 steps.
- **Price visibility** — $15–40 in intro. Good.
- **First come, first serve** — Creates urgency.
- **Group chat + bios** — Unique. Reduces pre-event anxiety.
- **Follow up without trading numbers** — Strong differentiator. Keep.
- **Free to apply** — Objection handled.

---

## 4. Rewrite Principles

1. **One idea per sentence** — Easier to scan.
2. **Lead with benefits, not caveats** — "Come solo" before "pay for food."
3. **Cut redundancy** — Food/drinks once (in intro, brief).
4. **Elevate differentiators** — Group chat and follow-up deserve emphasis.
5. **Shorter steps** — Step 3 split or tightened.
6. **No jargon** — "Invites that match" not "curated lists."
7. **Emotional payoff** — End on connection, not process.

---

## 5. Rewritten Copy

**Intro:**
Free to apply. Come solo — we guide introductions. Pay a service fee ($15–40) only when you reserve a spot. Your meal and drinks at the venue are on you.

**Step 1 — Apply**
One application. Tell us about yourself and what interests you. We send invites that match — no algorithm, just people and gatherings that fit.

**Step 2 — Get invited**
Weekly email and text invites to clubs and experiences. First come, first serve. Pay to reserve your spot.

**Step 3 — Show up**
Reserve your spot and you get a group chat with everyone attending. See who's going, read their bios, chat before you arrive. We guide introductions so meeting people feels natural. And the chat stays open — follow up without trading numbers.

---

## 6. Rationale for Rewrite

- **Intro:** Reordered — free, come solo, price, then food/drinks (neutral: "on you" not "you cover"). Shorter sentences.
- **Step 1:** Cut "curated lists." Added "no algorithm, just people and gatherings that fit" — differentiator, plain language.
- **Step 2:** Removed redundant food/drinks. Kept first come first serve. "Pay to reserve" — clear.
- **Step 3:** Split into 3 short sentences. Group chat first (the wow). Guided intros. "Chat stays open — follow up without trading numbers" as closing hook (emotional payoff).

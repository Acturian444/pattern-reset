## Part 5: Cross-Cutting Research Insights

### Intermittent Reinforcement (Neuroscience)

**Source:** B.F. Skinner, psychologyfor.com, self-care-haven.com

- Unpredictable rewards trigger dopamine spikes in **anticipation**—not just reward.
- Brain becomes obsessed with predicting the next "hit" → compulsive behavior.
- In relationships: occasional affection between coldness = addictive cycle.
- Heartbreak and rejection activate same brain regions as physical pain.
- Emotional withdrawal: oxytocin plummets, cortisol surges.
- "Humans feel far less incentive to continue behaviors that always yield rewards—but unpredictability makes us invest more effort."

**Use in:** Why This Feels So Confusing, Why You Stay, Situationship content.

---

### Attachment: Anxious + Avoidant (Research)

**Source:** PMC, Springer, attachment measures

- Two dimensions: anxiety + avoidance.
- Both predict lower relationship satisfaction, stability.
- Cross-partner effects: male avoidant → female satisfaction; female anxious → male stability.
- Pursuit-distance: anxious chases, avoidant withdraws. Classic pattern.
- "Different will feel wrong at first—that's your brain resisting change."

**Use in:** Why He Acts This Way, Your Response Pattern, combination insights.

---

### Why People Stay (Psychology Today)

**Source:** Psychology Today, SAGE journals

- Nervous system: known vs unknown (not healthy vs unhealthy). Bad = safer than uncertain.
- Sunk cost: invested time/effort makes leaving feel wasteful.
- Stress depletes cognitive resources for relationship decisions.
- "Nearly half of young adults experience relationship churning (on-again/off-again)."
- Love, attachment, identity = reasons to stay. Distinct from reasons to leave.

**Use in:** Why This Pattern Keeps Happening, Your Next Step.

---

### Mixed Signals (Psychology)

**Source:** Forreal.love, Reteuro, SELF

- Mixed signals = sender's internal conflict, not clear message about you.
- Low investment, attachment anxiety/avoidance, ambivalence, uncertainty.
- Intermittent reinforcement + anxious attachment + scarcity = more attractive.
- "Look at patterns over time, not single messages. Track three similar situations."

**Use in:** Mixed Signals pattern, Does He Like Me, What To Watch For.

---

### Relationship Anxiety (Cosmo 2025 Experts)

**Source:** Topham Sly, Rowett

- "Constant rumination—'Is that a red flag?' 'Do they love me?' 'Should I leave?'"
- Can stem from "nervous system reacting to past experiences of not feeling safe or wanted."
- Can exist in otherwise healthy relationships.
- "What helps: learning to understand and regulate those feelings, so you can reach for your partner with clarity and calm, rather than reacting from fear."
- "Stop following relationship accounts on social media. Doomscrolling won't help."

**Use in:** Am I Overthinking validation, Why This Feels So Confusing.

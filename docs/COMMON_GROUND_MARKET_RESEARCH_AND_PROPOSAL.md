# Common Ground — Market Research & Proposal

> Consumer behavior, competition, culture, and product analysis with recommendations for the Clubs section and overall site.

---

## 1. Consumer Behavior

### The Friendship Recession
- **12%** of U.S. adults have no close friends (up from 3% in 1990)
- Time with friends dropped from **60 min/day** to **26 min/day** in 20 years
- Weekly friend time fell from **6.5 hours** to **4 hours** (2014–2019)
- People increasingly do activities **alone** (eating, watching TV) rather than with friends

### What Drives Conversion
- **Emotional outcomes** (overcoming isolation, building friendships) outperform transactional benefits
- **Clarity** beats creativity — users need obvious signals
- **Trust** through structure, transparency, and streamlined navigation
- **Low friction** — too many questions cause 30–50% abandonment
- **Mobile-first** — 60%+ of registrations happen on mobile

### Decision Psychology
- People process **cards 34% faster** than plain lists
- **Repeated exposure** (mere exposure effect) drives friendship formation
- **Third place rituals** create belonging — predictable time + place + familiar faces
- **Genuine scarcity** (e.g., "Limited to 14 people") builds trust; artificial FOMO backfires

## 2. Competition

### Direct Competitors
| Product | Model | Price | Key Differentiator |
|---------|-------|-------|--------------------|
| **Friending** | App, ID-verified | Free/freemium | 100% verification, "Are You In?" cards |
| **We3** | App, group matching | Free | Groups of 3, psychographic quiz |
| **Parlor Social Club** | Application-based | Membership | Curated calendar, 4.8 rating |
| **Locals** | Clubs + events | Varies | Creator-led, community organizers |
| **PerfectFriends** | Subscription | $46–$700/yr | Member-driven events, VIP benefits |
| **Meetup** | Event discovery | Free + fees | Broad, local groups |
| **Eventbrite** | Ticketing | Per-event | Micro-events growing 23% |

### Competitive Gaps Common Ground Fills
- **Curated, not algorithmic** — Human-curated vs. app matching
- **Ritual structure** — Same time, same place (not one-off events)
- **Pay-per-attend** — No high subscription; $15–40 when you go
- **Bay Area focus** — Local, not national
- **Sober-friendly** — Not bar-centric
- **Small groups** — 10–14 people, not crowds

## 3. Culture — Bay Area

### Loneliness Crisis
- San Mateo County declared loneliness a **public health emergency** (2024)
- **46%** of Bay Area workers remote — loss of office social connection
- Tech workers face job anxiety, existential dread, isolation
- "Friendship clubs" and third spaces emerging (e.g., The Commons, Hayes Valley)

### Target Audience Signals
- Moved to Bay Area, want to meet people faster
- Tired of apps, want real-life connection
- Prefer meaningful conversation over bars/small talk
- Want weekend activities with interesting people
- Social circle has shrunk; want to rebuild

### Cultural Fit
- **Third place ritual** resonates — church, trivia night, run club
- **"Same time. Same place. New people each week"** matches local desire for structure + novelty
- **Curated** beats overwhelming event feeds
- **Apply to join** signals quality and safety (Parlor, elite clubs use this)

## 5. Proposal: Our Clubs Section

### A. Club Cards — Add

| Element | Recommendation | Why |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| **Image** | One photo per club (walk, dinner, music) | 34% faster processing; emotional connection |
| **Price hint** | "$15–40 when you attend" (or per-club range) | Reduces uncertainty; competitors show pricing |
| **"Best for" badge** | Keep taglines, make them badges | "Best for first-time members" etc. — helps self-select |
| **Consistent order** | Name → Schedule → City → Capacity → Description → CTA | Scannability; users compare without reading everything |

### B. Club Cards — Edit

| Current | Proposed | Why |
|---------|----------|-----|
| "Request Invite" | "Apply to Join" or "Get Invites" | Matches nav CTA; clearer action |
| Subtitle (Walk · Coffee · Conversation) | Keep, ensure it's scannable | Good; consider slightly larger |
| Long description | Trim to 1–2 sentences; benefit-first | Cards encourage clicks, don't explain everything |

### C. Club Cards — Remove

| Element | Recommendation | Why |
|---------|----------------|-----|
| Format line (~90 min · Walk + Coffee) | **Consider removing** or folding into description | Redundant; adds length; capacity + schedule matter more |
| Italic tagline at bottom | **Keep** but style as badge | "Best for first-time members" is valuable |

### D. Section-Level Additions

| Addition | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **Intro line above cards** | "Three clubs. Same time every week. Apply once, get invites." |
| **Micro-FAQ** | 1–2 lines: "How do invites work?" → "We send them weekly. First come, first serve." |
| **Social proof (when available)** | "Join 50+ members" or "New members every week" |
| **Reassurance** | Keep "New clubs launch regularly. Members can join multiple clubs." |

## 6. Proposal: Site-Wide Edits

### Add
- **FAQ accordion** — "How do invites work?" "What does it cost?" "Is it really for making friends?"
- **Testimonial placeholder** — One quote when you have it ("My friends from the Saturday Walk")
- **Price visibility** — Mention $15–40 in hero or clubs intro, not just in steps

### Edit
- **Hero banner** — "Meet 10 New People Every Week" — validate; may feel like a promise. Consider "Meet New People Every Week" or tie to clubs specifically
- **Apply form** — Audit fields; reduce if possible (30–50% abandon on long forms)
- **Steps section** — Add "Pay $15–40 when you reserve" more prominently

### Remove or Simplify
- **Redundant copy** — "Apply once. Get ongoing invitations" appears in multiple places; consolidate
- **Accordion depth** — Ensure "For You" and "About" bullets stay scannable; cut if too long

## 8. Summary

**Market position:** Common Ground sits in a strong niche — curated, ritual-based, pay-per-attend, Bay Area-focused. Competitors are either app-heavy (Friending, We3), subscription-heavy (PerfectFriends), or event-marketplace (Meetup, Eventbrite). The "same time, same place" ritual and small-group model are differentiated.

**Consumer fit:** Bay Area loneliness + remote work + desire for real connection align with the offer. Apply-to-join builds trust; pay-per-attend lowers commitment.

**Clubs section:** Add imagery and price visibility, tighten copy, add light FAQ. Keep structure (Clubs vs. Experiences), taglines, and reassurance. Remove or simplify redundant format lines.

**Site-wide:** More price visibility, shorter apply form if possible, FAQ for trust. Add social proof and testimonials as you get them.

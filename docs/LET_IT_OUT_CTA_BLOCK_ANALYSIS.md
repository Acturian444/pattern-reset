# Let It Out — CTA Block Expert & Audience Analysis
## How to Improve the Post-Form Copy Block

**Current block (order on page):**
> Something you've been holding in.  
> A truth you've never said out loud.  
> What pattern might be behind your story?  
> Discover Your Pattern →  
> Feeling overwhelmed?

**Context:** This appears below the Share Anonymously button on the Let It Out write form. Audience: women 24–38, relationship-healing, emotionally intelligent. Goals: (1) prime what to write, (2) funnel to quiz, (3) offer support.

---

## Expert Roundtable Input

### Eugene Schwartz (Copywriting Legend)
**Verdict: Hierarchy is wrong. One primary CTA.**
"You're asking for three things at once: write, take the quiz, get support. The reader's mind can only hold one dominant desire. Right now the quiz funnel competes with the write prompt. The info text ('Something you've been holding in...') should prime the textarea—it belongs above the form, not below the button. Below the button, the primary action is done. That's when you offer the next step: quiz. Support should be secondary, de-emphasized."

**Suggested order:** Move "Something you've been holding in..." above the form. Below the button: Quiz funnel first, support link smaller/secondary.

---

### Emily Watson (Conversion Copywriter)
**Verdict: Simplify the quiz funnel. Support link needs warmth.**
"'What pattern might be behind your story?' is a good hook but feels like an ad. 'Discover Your Pattern' is generic. Try: 'What pattern is running your story?' or 'Find the pattern behind it →'—shorter, more direct. 'Feeling overwhelmed?' is fine but could feel clinical. 'Need support?' or 'Struggling? You're not alone.' adds warmth. Also: the info text below the button is confusing. If it's meant to prime the write, it's in the wrong place."

**Suggested:** Quiz: *"What pattern is behind your story?"* + *"Find Your Pattern →"* | Support: *"Need support? You're not alone."*

---

### Olivia Chen (Hook & Copy Specialist)
**Verdict: Quiz funnel competes with the moment.**
"Someone is about to share something vulnerable. You're immediately pivoting to 'take our quiz.' That's a mood whiplash. The quiz CTA works better after they've posted—in the confirmation modal. On the form page, let the focus be: write, share, support. Move the quiz funnel to post-submit only, or make it much subtler (small text, not a prominent link)."

**Suggested:** Remove quiz funnel from form page. Keep only: info text (move above form) + support link. Quiz funnel stays in confirmation modal.

---

### Jason Lee (Quiz-to-Product Bridge Specialist)
**Verdict: Right idea, wrong placement.**
"The quiz funnel belongs where someone has just completed an emotional release—that's the confirmation moment. On the form, they haven't shared yet. You're asking them to split attention. Keep the form page clean: write prompt, Share button, support. Quiz funnel: confirmation modal only. If you must have it on the form, make it one small line: 'Curious about your pattern? Take the quiz →'—below the fold, low pressure."

**Suggested:** Form page: info + button + support. Quiz: confirmation only, or one subtle line on form.

---

### Ryan Foster (Checkout & UX Optimizer)
**Verdict: Too many CTAs. Reduce cognitive load.**
"Three distinct CTAs (share, quiz, support) = decision fatigue. Primary: Share. Secondary: Support (safety net). Tertiary: Quiz (can live in confirmation). The info text below the button doesn't serve a clear purpose—users have already seen the form. Either move it above the textarea as the prompt, or remove it. 'Feeling overwhelmed?' is good—it's a safety valve. Keep it visible but not competing."

**Suggested:** One primary CTA (Share). Support link subtle. Quiz in confirmation. Info text above form if kept.

---

### Nina Patel (Brand Voice Coach)
**Verdict: Tone consistency.**
"'Something you've been holding in. A truth you've never said out loud.'—warm, inviting. 'What pattern might be behind your story?'—curiosity, good. 'Discover Your Pattern'—feels like marketing. 'Feeling overwhelmed?'—caring but brief. The quiz CTA is the odd one out; it breaks the intimate tone. Soften it: 'Curious what pattern might be at play? Find out →' or keep it only in the confirmation where the pivot makes sense."

**Suggested:** Quiz CTA: *"Curious what pattern might be at play? Find out →"* or move to confirmation only.

---

### Dr. Brené Brown (Psychology / Vulnerability)
**Verdict: Support before quiz. Safety first.**
"The person writing may be in a vulnerable state. 'Feeling overwhelmed?' should feel like a lifeline, not an afterthought. Consider moving it higher—near the form, not at the bottom. The quiz can wait. Someone who's overwhelmed needs to see support before they see 'take our quiz.' Order matters: safety, then curiosity."

**Suggested:** Support link above or adjacent to quiz funnel. Or: Support first, quiz second. Language: *"Feeling overwhelmed? You're not alone."*

---

### Dr. Sofia Martinez (Trauma-Informed Psychologist)
**Verdict: Support visibility.**
"'Feeling overwhelmed?' is good—direct, non-stigmatizing. But it's last and small. For someone in distress, it should be easy to find. Consider: larger type, or a dedicated 'Need support?' section. The quiz funnel doesn't need to be on the form at all—it can feel like you're monetizing their vulnerability. Post-submit is the right moment."

**Suggested:** Elevate support. De-emphasize or move quiz to confirmation.

## Recommended Changes

### 1. Move info text above the form
Place "Something you've been holding in. A truth you've never said out loud." above the textarea as the write prompt. Remove from below the button.

### 2. Form page (below Share button): Simplify
- **Keep:** Support link — "Feeling overwhelmed?" (or "Need support? You're not alone.")
- **Remove or de-emphasize:** Quiz funnel on form page. Move to confirmation modal only.

### 3. If quiz funnel stays on form page
- Make it one subtle line: *"Curious what pattern might be at play? Find out →"*
- Place it below support, smaller/lighter styling
- Or: *"What pattern might be behind your story? Take the quiz →"* — but only if confirmation modal also has it

### 4. Support link improvements
- **Option A:** *"Feeling overwhelmed? You're not alone."* — adds warmth
- **Option B:** *"Need support?"* — shorter, less heavy
- **Option C:** Keep "Feeling overwhelmed?" — it's direct and works

### 5. Confirmation modal (post-submit)
- Keep quiz funnel here as primary next step: "What pattern might be behind your story?" + "Discover Your Pattern →"
- This is the right moment—they've just released; curiosity is natural

---

## Final Recommendation

**Form page (below Share Anonymously):**
- Support link only: *"Feeling overwhelmed?"* or *"Feeling overwhelmed? You're not alone."*
- Quiz funnel: remove from form; keep in confirmation modal only

**Above textarea:**
- Add: *"Something you've been holding in. A truth you've never said out loud."*

**Confirmation modal:**
- Keep: "What pattern might be behind your story?" + "Discover Your Pattern →"

This creates a clear flow: prime → write → share → (optional) support. Quiz appears only after they've shared, when the pivot to "what pattern?" feels natural.
